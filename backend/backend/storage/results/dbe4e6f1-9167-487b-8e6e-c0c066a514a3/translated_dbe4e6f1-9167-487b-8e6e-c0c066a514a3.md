Bank of Refusals  
63 Examples  
by Weeks and Types

---

The bank is used in two ways: the participant scrolls through the page and recognizes themselves — this lowers the barrier to the first refusal.

Week 1 – Tactical Level

Refusal of specific actions today

Meetings and Calls

1. Cancelled the weekly team meeting to discuss the current status of affairs — replaced it with an asynchronous voice message.

2. Did not attend a meeting that could have been accomplished with a 5-minute phone call.

3. Declined a call where I was invited to “just listen.”

4. Rescheduled a meeting with a contractor — there was no specific issue, so the meeting was premature.

5. Refused to be “on call” on a day off.

---

6. I canceled all meetings starting before 10 a.m. — that’s my work time.

7. I refused to have lunch with a person from whom I expect nothing and to whom I won’t be able to help.

Tasks and Operations

8. I did not respond to an incoming request myself — I forwarded it to the manager.

9. I crossed out a task from the list that had been postponed for three weeks: if I haven’t done it in three weeks, it means it’s not needed.

10. I did not create a presentation where an email would suffice.

11. I refused to personally check a report that the team had already reviewed.

12. I did not try to “improve” a result that was already good enough.

---

13. Removed viewing the news from my morning schedule — replaced it with 10 minutes of silence.

14. Did not reply to a message

The message arrived instantly — responded 3 hours later, nothing happened.

Communication

15. Did not write a detailed explanation where a simple “no” would suffice.

16. Refused to chat where a two-minute call would be enough.

17. Did not justify a decision I had already made.

18. Left a group chat where I was just an observer.

19. Did not reply to an email that didn’t require my response.

---

20. Refused to comment on someone else's decision that doesn’t concern me.

21. Didn’t write “Understood, I will do this” under a task I don’t intend to complete.

Week 2 – Structural level. Refusal of regular obligations that systematically drain resources.

Regular Formats

22. Eliminated a weekly meeting where no decisions have been made in the past two months.

23. Refused to do a monthly report that nobody reads, and proposed an alternative — 24 hours.

24. Closed a project that was “almost done” for half a year.

25. Removed calls with a client who doesn't make decisions from the schedule.

26. Politely refused to participate in a working group I joined six months ago.

Needed time to process the team's lack of openness.

28. Removed the morning “status update” from the general chat — no one reacted, I did it for myself.

Delegation of Authority and Role Distribution

29. Stopped personally approving all invoices up to 50,000 — delegated this task to the finance department.

30. Refused to be the only contact person for a key client — introduced a manager.

31. Stopped writing social media posts myself — passed final decisions to the editor.

32. Excluded myself from the hiring approval chain for positions below manager.

33. Refused to conduct personal negotiations with minor contractors.

---

34. Stopped participating in planning personal life at work — it’s not in my job description.

35. Removed myself from all team mailings — “just to stay informed.”

External Obligations

36. Refused to speak at an event that doesn’t match my priorities this quarter.

37. Did not renew membership in an organization where I haven’t been active for the past eight months.

38. Declined a partnership offer that is “interesting in theory” but doesn’t contribute to business development.

39. Removed from my annual list a conference I attend out of habit rather than because of a set limit of 40 years.

40. Refused to be a mentor for someone else — I already have a limit of 41 years.

41. Did not agree to join the board of directors of a company where I do not see my role.

42. Refused to collaborate on something that takes my time but only benefits the other person.

Week 3 – Identity Level

Rejection of beliefs, roles, and behavioral patterns that determine behavior.

Beliefs About Control

43. Stopped being the person who must know everything going on in the company.

44. Rejected the belief: “If you want it done well, do it yourself.”

45. Stopped checking the work of people I trust — trust does not require verification.

46. Removed from my system the “final say” in matters where my team is more competent.

47. Stopped redoing things for others that were done “not how I would do them myself.”

---

Ideas About Availability

48. Refused the role of “always available” — set unavailable hours and informed the team.

49. Stopped replying to messages during the first hour after waking up.

50. Got rid of the belief that not responding quickly means letting someone down.

51. Gave up the habit of “monitoring” all chats — stopped reading what doesn’t require any action.

52. Stopped taking my phone to every meeting — being present is more important than being available.

Beliefs About Growth

53. Refused a new direction that “could work out” — I already have something that works.

54. Stopped saying “yes” to everything.

Interesting — interesting does not mean important.

---

55. Removed from the strategy a goal that sounds attractive but isn’t mine.

56. Refused to hire one more person to the team — the problem is not with the people, but with the processes.

57. Stopped scaling what still doesn’t work consistently.

Beliefs About Relationships

58. Stopped maintaining relationships with people whose interactions drain my energy.

59. Stopped explaining my decisions to those who didn’t ask.

60. Removed from life the duty of “being useful” to everyone who turns to it.

61. Refused the role of the “problem solver” for those who don’t want to solve their problems themselves.

62. Stopped apologizing for boundaries I set for myself.

---

63. Refuse relationships where I am always the initiator — if it’s important to the other person, they’ll write themselves.

How to Use the Bank

If it's hard to understand in the morning what is pushing you away — open the bank and find what resonates with you. This is not a to-do list, but a mirror. By recognizing yourself, you understand what pushes you away.
